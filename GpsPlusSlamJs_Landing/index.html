GPS+SLAM

One URL. No app. Real‑world AR.

GPS+SLAM turns the mobile browser into a window that pins digital content to real places — streets, trails, squares — and keeps it there.

Scroll to walk in ↓

A printed code is the door — and the anchor.

Visitors scan a QR code on a sign. The experience opens instantly — and it knows exactly where it stands from the very first second.

Raw GPS wobbles. Fusion holds still.

GPS alone jitters by meters. Fused with the phone's own motion tracking, your content stays put — and gets steadier the longer visitors walk.

This is what your visitors see.

Their own phone becomes the window. No headset, no download — arrows, labels and stories laid over the real world.

Works anywhere. Fully offline.

No cloud mapping, no scanning vans, no pre-scanned city centers. Everything aligns on the device — on a plaza or in a random park.

What will you build?

Guided trails. Talking statues. Treasure hunts. Anything you can pin to a place.

Free to start. No signup.

The framework is Apache-2.0, with a free community license for the alignment core bundled in. No API key. No account.
