Location-based WebXR

GPS alone is too inaccurate for AR.

Meters of wobble ruin the illusion. Location-based WebXR fuses it with the phone's own motion tracking — and 3D content finally sticks to real places. In the mobile browser, from a single URL.

  • No app — one URL
  • GPS + WebXR fusion: anchors that hold still
  • Fully offline — no cloud mapping
  • Any QR code = door + instant position fix

Any ordinary QR code is the door — and the anchor.

One scan and the page jumps straight into AR, no install. And the code can double as an instant, precise first fix — before GPS has even warmed up.

Raw GPS wobbles. Fusion holds still.

Every GPS reading lands somewhere else. Averaging them nails the spot — and fusion with WebXR tracking keeps it nailed while the user moves.

This is what your users see.

Their own phone becomes the window. No headset, no download — arrows, labels and stories laid over the real world.

Works anywhere. Fully offline.

No cloud mapping, no scanning vans, no pre-scanned city centers. Everything aligns on the device — on a plaza, in a random park, or in an enchanted forest where a dragon waits.

What will you build?

Anything that needs precise global positioning, runs offline, and places 3D content in the physical world:

  • Guided tours & city walks with content at the real spots
  • Campus, venue & trade-fair navigation
  • Construction & architecture previews at 1:1 scale
  • Underground utilities made visible on site
  • Safety training & emergency drills on location
